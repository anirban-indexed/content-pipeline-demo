## Target audience

Primary: Operations directors, plant managers, heads of continuous improvement, HR and organisational capability leads, and senior management at mid-to-large Australian organisations actively evaluating operational improvement investment.

Secondary: Team leaders and supervisors being considered for Lean Six Sigma certification, and finance and strategy executives who need to understand the business case for improvement programmes.

These readers evaluate consulting and training investments on measurable outcomes, ROI, organisational fit, and the credibility of methodology. They are sceptical of vendor claims and hype. They trust frameworks, balanced perspectives, evidence from credible research bodies, and frank acknowledgement of when investment is not appropriate.

## Content purpose

Content serves three goals:
1. Rank for operational excellence, continuous improvement, and Lean Six Sigma keywords in Australian search
2. Build authority with decision-makers at the evaluation stage — convincing readers who are comparing options or building an internal business case
3. Convert informed visitors into consultation requests by demonstrating OE Partners' depth of expertise and honest, outcome-focused approach

## Hard rules — non-negotiable

- Write in British English throughout: organisation, programme, colour, recognise, optimise, practise (verb), licence (noun), favour, behaviour, analyse
- Never position certification as a professional development box to tick — always frame as a strategic capability investment tied to operational outcomes
- Never state specific ROI percentages or productivity improvement figures without attribution to a verified, named study or report from an authoritative source
- Do NOT name competing Australian consulting or training firms
- Em dashes are prohibited — use a comma or restructure the sentence
- DMAIC, Lean Six Sigma belt levels, and methodology names are accurate and consistent with APMG-International standards
- Every performance claim about training or consulting ROI must be qualified: "organisations typically," "when properly deployed," "evidence suggests"
- Articles must acknowledge both when the investment is appropriate AND when it is not — the honest trade-off framing is non-negotiable
