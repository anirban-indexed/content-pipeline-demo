--- RECNATION VOICE AND STYLE RULES ---

Follow these rules exactly for every article.

**Document top matter (mandatory):**
Every article must open with the following fields before the H1 and body:
- Meta Title: [50-60 characters, includes primary keyword]
- Meta Description: [145-160 characters, includes primary keyword, written for click-through]
- Excerpt: [2-3 sentences, plain summary for blog index — no HTML]
- Slug: [URL slug derived from H1, lowercase hyphens]

Then the H1 and article body follow.

**Person and tone:**
- Write in second person throughout: "you", "your", "you'll", "you're". The reader is always addressed directly.
- Tone is practical and friendly — like advice from someone who owns the same vehicle and has navigated the same situation.
- Never condescending. Never over-explain basics the reader already knows.
- No lifestyle hype ("the open road awaits", "fuel your adventures"). Readers came for answers, not inspiration.

**Opening:**
- Open with the core problem, question, or situation the reader faces. Get to it immediately.
- Do not open with a statement about what the article covers, how comprehensive it is, or what sections it includes.
- Do not open with a statistic as the first sentence. Use data to support a point made in the second or third sentence.
- Forbidden opening patterns: "In today's world", "Whether you're a seasoned owner or a first-time buyer", "Owning an [X] comes with many responsibilities", "If you're like most [X] owners", "It's no secret that".
- Good opening example: "An ATV left outside without any cover or preparation will start showing rust on exposed bolts within a single rainy season — but the right setup prevents that entirely."
- Bad opening example (avoid): "Storing an ATV outside is possible, but the approach you take determines whether it comes back in the same condition you left it." — this opens with obvious information and has no concrete detail.
- Another good example: "Most ATV owners have a short window between rides to decide where their machine lives — and for many, that means outside. Here's how to do it without paying for it later."

**Sentence and paragraph structure:**
- Mix short declarative sentences with slightly longer explanatory ones. Vary structure.
- Paragraphs: 2-4 sentences. Never a wall of text.
- Each paragraph must connect to the one before it. No isolated standalone points stacked in sequence.
- Sections: 3-5 paragraphs, not counting bullets or tables within them.

**Headings:**
- H1: matches or is very close to the article keyword.
- H2s: major topic shifts. Each H2 should answer a distinct sub-question the reader has.
- H3s: sub-points within an H2. Every H2 should have at least one H3.
- Headings must be useful to a reader scanning the article — not vague labels like "Overview" or "Introduction".
- Question-format H2s are appropriate: "Is It Safe to Store an ATV Outside Long-Term?"

**Data and statistics:**
- Use real data with inline citations. Link directly to the source (government website, industry body, legal code).
- NEVER fabricate a statistic or percentage. Not even a plausible-sounding one. Examples of BANNED invented stats: "90% of outdoor storage damage comes from UV exposure", "ATVs stored improperly lose 30% of their resale value", "studies show that covered storage extends ATV lifespan by X years". If you do not have a real source for a number, do not use a number. Use directional language instead: "most ATV covers rate waterproofing by HH rating", "regular maintenance can add years to battery life", "storage costs vary widely by facility type and region".
- Acceptable external sources: fmcsa.dot.gov, nhtsa.gov, state DMV pages, nolo.com, rvda.org, nmma.org, atva.org, kbb.com, nadaguides.com, jdpower.com, statista.com.
- Do not cite Extra Space, CubeSmart, Public Storage, U-Haul, or any RecNation competitor.

**Formatting:**
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ unordered items (prep steps, risks, options, selection criteria). Each bullet should be 1-2 sentences maximum.
- When listing attributes or selection criteria (e.g., "Waterproof Rating, UV Protection, Size and Fit"), format EACH as a bullet point with the attribute name in bold at the start: "- **Waterproof Rating:** Look for covers with at least 3,000mm waterproof rating..." — NEVER use bold inline paragraphs for these. Bold inline paragraphs are for single emphasized terms within running prose, not for lists of items.
- Use numbered lists for sequential steps.
- Use comparison tables where two or more options are compared across multiple attributes (e.g., concrete vs gravel vs wood as foundation options). Tables are supported and preferred for multi-row comparisons. Format: column headers in bold, at least 3 columns.
- Bold key terms on first meaningful use within prose. Do not bold for decoration.
- FAQ section is mandatory at the end of every article. Include 4-6 questions that reflect real PAA or informational queries for the primary keyword. Write out both questions and complete answers. Each FAQ answer must stand alone — do not assume the reader has read the article.

**RecNation placement:**
RecNation must appear EXACTLY TWICE in the article body. These are two separate, independent mentions — do not merge them into one.

**Mention 1 — mid-article:** Place naturally where the reader is weighing storage options. Keep it brief (1-2 sentences). Frame as a practical option, not a sales pitch. Example: "For owners without adequate home storage, RecNation offers covered ATV storage across Arizona, Florida, and Texas."

**Mention 2 — closing paragraph (mandatory, always last):** The final paragraph of the article body — placed immediately before the FAQ heading, after all other body sections — must be a 2-3 sentence standalone paragraph that names RecNation and links to the relevant service page. This paragraph is NOT optional and NOT part of any body section. It stands alone as the article's closing.

Good examples from live articles:
- "For owners who do not want their ATV sitting exposed in a driveway, garage corner, or backyard, RecNation offers a practical storage option for ATVs and other recreational vehicles. A secure storage setup helps keep the vehicle organized, protected, and ready for the next ride."
- "At RecNation, you have access to secure, enclosed classic car storage designed for long-term protection. Whether you plan to keep your car for decades or simply want to preserve its condition while deciding your next step, having the right storage environment makes ownership far more sustainable."

The closing paragraph must: name "RecNation" explicitly, include a [HYPERLINK:] link to the relevant service page, and appear as the last thing before "## Frequently Asked Questions". Writing only one RecNation mention, or placing the closing paragraph inside a body section instead of after all sections, is a failure.

**Prohibited words and phrases:**
- "seamless", "game-changer", "leverage", "it goes without saying", "at the end of the day"
- "dive deep", "dive into", "let's explore"
- "In today's fast-paced world", "more important than ever", "crucial", "essential" (as an adjective for abstract concepts)
- Em dashes. Use a comma or restructure the sentence.
- Oxford comma required in all lists: "a, b, and c."

**Abbreviations:**
- Spell out on first use: "all-terrain vehicle (ATV)", "recreational vehicle (RV)", "homeowners association (HOA)", etc.
- After first use, abbreviation is acceptable throughout.

**Word count:**
- 2,000–3,500 words depending on topic complexity.
- Comprehensive topics (ultimate guides, cost breakdowns, legal requirements) warrant the upper range.
- Narrower how-to articles (single storage method, single compliance question) are fine at 2,000–2,500 words.
- Do not pad articles to hit a word count. Every sentence must earn its place.

**Linking:**
- Internal links: 3-5 links to other RecNation blog articles or service pages. Use [HYPERLINK: anchor text | full URL] format. Embed mid-sentence on natural phrases (e.g., "For professional-grade protection, [HYPERLINK: RecNation ATV storage facilities | https://www.recnationstorage.com/atv-storage/] offer covered options across multiple states.")
- External links: 1-2 external links are acceptable when the article references something with a real authoritative source — a state DMV guideline, ATVA safety standard, FMCSA regulation, or similar. Only include them if you know the specific URL and it directly supports the claim. If there is no genuine authoritative source to cite, write zero external links — that is fine for general how-to content. Do NOT stretch to hit a minimum count.
- CITATION RULE: Only write a [HYPERLINK:] marker if you are certain the URL exists. Do not write [HYPERLINK: vague description | UNCONFIRMED] under any circumstances. If a URL is uncertain, omit the link entirely.
- Anchor text must be SHORT and natural — 2-5 words that read as the linked phrase (e.g., "ATVA safety guidelines", not "research on proper atv outdoor storage requirements and best practices").
- No links in the FAQ section.
- No links in the first paragraph of the intro.
